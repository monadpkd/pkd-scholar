# Style Revision Prompt

Revise the following draft entry for the PKD Scholar dictionary.

Apply these rules:

## Tone
- Analytical, not devotional or fan-explanatory.
- College-level: clear for an educated non-specialist.
- Confident but not overclaiming.

## Structure
- Lead with definition, then PKD relevance.
- Keep evidentiary layers visibly separate ("In the fiction..." / "In the Exegesis..." / "In criticism...").
- Include contradictions if they exist.
- End with See Also links in three directions (concepts, works, scholars).

## Fix These If Present
- Remove "clearly," "obviously," "fascinatingly," "interestingly," "it is worth noting"
- Remove "PKD was ahead of his time" or similar hagiography
- Remove anachronistic AI relevance ("in today's world of AI...")
- Remove diagnosis ("Dick was schizophrenic / paranoid / mentally ill")
- Replace passive voice pileups with active constructions
- Replace scare quotes with definitions
- Add citations where claims lack them
- Convert plot summaries into analytical observations

## Do Not
- Add content that isn't supported by the evidence packet
- Invent citations
- Merge evidentiary lanes (fiction evidence into Exegesis claims, etc.)
- Remove contradictions to make the entry sound more unified

Output the revised entry in full, with a brief changelog at the end listing what was changed and why.
